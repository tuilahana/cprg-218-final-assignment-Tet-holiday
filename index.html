Tet, or Tết Nguyên Đán, is the Vietnamese New Year, also known as the Spring Festival or Lunar New Year. Tet's date varies annually since it is based on the Vietnamese lunar calendar, usually occurring in late January or early February on the Gregorian calendar. Tet is the most important celebration in Vietnam, marking the Lunar New Year. It is a time for family reunions, honoring ancestors, and welcoming prosperity.
